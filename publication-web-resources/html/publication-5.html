This button-up shirt explores the relation-ship between the hu-man body and organ-ic life forms. Inspired by anatomical studies of the ribcage and the fluid move-ment of jellyfish, the design translates skeletal structures into decorative stitching run-ning along the front of the garment. These lines echo the protective yet delicate framework of bones, creating a dialogue between structure and softness.

The open back introduces a sense of vul-nerability, contrasting the structured front while emphasizing the nat-ural lines of the body. The ac-companying trousers further de-velop this organic inspiration, with a lace pattern that reflects the intricate textures and flowing structures found in jellyfish. As in my other designs, natural forms play a key role in the design process, allowing organic structures to inform both silhouette and surface details.
