This midi skirt was the first garment I developed at ESMOD and acted as my introduc-tion to translating visual research into pattern work. Starting from a mood-board of sea-shell macro-photography, sea-spray and wind-billowed fabric, I focused on the shell’s stepped ridges and the way coastal breeze lifts light material. To echo those ridges, I drafted a series of graduated cotton flounces that spiral from the waist to the hem, producing measured volume without resorting to heavy gath-ers. The main fabric is a tightly woven cotton twill: firm enough to hold each ruffle’s edge, yet soft enough to move easily around the body. A deep brown, matched to Pantone 19-0912 “Brown Chocolate,” grounds the marine reference in an earthier palette and makes the skirt wearable beyond the con-cept exercise. Side-seam pockets and a clean waistband keep the structure functional, while a narrow rolled hem prevents fraying along the curved flounces. The finished piece demonstrates how a single natural motif—here, the ripple of a shell—can drive decisions in color, cut, and construction, balancing conceptual clarity with everyday practicality.
